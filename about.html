What is congressional redistricting? Congressional redistricting is the process of redrawing district boundaries when a state has more representatives than districts. Redistricting occurs every ten years, with the national census. Our System: Our system enables users to view prior congressional and polling district boundaries and demographics of U.S states. In addition, an individual can register as a user to see a potential redistricting map of a particular year with specific constraints. To ensure certain constraints are met, a congressional redistricting scenario computes an objective function that will provide a number, measuring the goodness of a district. Our system allows individuals to strategize redistricting while limiting the political advantages of gerrymandering.
